About rainforest

Rainforests are biota that receive a lot of rainfall. Most of the rainforests are close to the equator, and there are large areas of rainforests in Africa, Asia and South America where the equator passes. The humid climate ensures rapid production of trees and plants. Known as the "Lungs of the Earth", the Amazon rainforest provides about 20% of the earth's oxygen and absorbs more than one billion tons of atmospheric carbon dioxide every year. Therefore, the Amazon rainforest plays a role in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and reducing the impact of global warming. Important natural regulating areas, crucial to controlling global warming.

Species diversity

From the most basic breathing and survival to the most gorgeous wings and scenery, biological diversity presents the diverse features of all species on the earth. The Amazon is one of the most biologically diverse ecosystems in the world, giving birth to more than three million species, including one-third of the world’s tropical tree species. However, as human activities invade the rainforest, deforestation, logging, and fires continue to occur, and species The rate of extinction has increased dramatically, and biodiversity is under threat.
